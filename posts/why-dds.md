# Why I'm pursuing a DDS

I trained as a dentist. I learned to read an x-ray, to calm a nervous patient,
to work with my hands in a space the size of a coin. Completing my BDS and a
one-year clinical internship only deepened one feeling: this is the work I want
to do for the rest of my life.

## What volunteering taught me

Alongside the clinic, I volunteer in **public health**. Sitting with data and
communities — rather than just individual patients — showed me how much oral
health is shaped by access, habits, and inequality. It made me a more thoughtful
clinician, and it made the case for prevention impossible to ignore.

Here's what I keep coming back to:

1. Good care starts long before someone sits in the chair.
2. A clinician who understands populations can help more than one patient at a time.
3. The disparities in oral health are solvable — with the right training and will.

## The goal

That's why I'm driven toward an **advanced-standing DDS**: to sharpen my clinical
skills in the U.S. system and serve diverse patient populations with care that's
both hands-on and evidence-based.
